Landscape

The pursuit of clean electricity is for the fundamental purpose of improving Earth's health. Climate Change is an increasing world problem and this threat is growing every passing year. Earth's Climate is generated by four components:

Atmosphere

The air we breathe

Biosphere

The plants and animals

Hydrosphere

The Planet's Water

Cryosphere

The glaciers and Ice sheets

Our emissions of CO2 into the atmosphere had started to modify every one of these components.  Ocean acidification and temperature increase had started to modify our hydrosphere. Rising atmospheric and the sea-surface temperature had started to modify the cryosphere, the rapid melting of the ice sheets. These spheres are reverting back towards the biosphere, which are impacting ecosystems. This is correlating towards one of the largest extinctions rates to recently occur in years.

In return, it is also reverting back towards our own activities as a species by affecting our greatest asset - The Global Economy. The World Economic Forum outlines that Climate Change is the number one risk to the global economy. The Insurance Information Institute has monitored the losses over the years from natural catastrophes and the trend is evidently increasing. In the year of 2016, the overall losses worldwide for natural disasters were $175 billion, up from $103 billion in 2015. The flash floods in Europe from May to June, caused $3.2 billion in losses (22 fatalities), Floods in the United States in August resulted in losses of $3.0 billion (13 Fatalities); and the Canadian wildfire, Fort McMurray, in May caused $2.9 billion Losses.
